  • NDVI vegetation health analysis across 2019-2022
  • Soil composition analysis and classification

Drought Resilience in Native and Non-Native Tree Species

This research examines the drought resilience of three tree species in Southern Germany using dendroecological methods:

  • Compared growth responses of Douglas Fir, Norway Spruce, and Pedunculate Oak to the 1976 and 2003 droughts
  • Analyzed long-term climatic influences on tree growth using response function analysis
  • Evaluated climate adaptation potential of non-native species (Douglas Fir) vs. native species
  • Applied statistical validation using R-squared values and Wilcoxon rank-sum tests

The findings suggest Pedunculate Oak shows the highest drought resilience, while Douglas Fir demonstrates improving adaptation over time, making it a potential alternative for climate-resilient forestry.
